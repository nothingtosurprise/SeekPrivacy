<p><b>SeekPrivacy creates a shield.</b> Apps like social media and basic tools demand "Storage Access"—if you refuse, they break. If you accept, they spy. This creates a painful trade-off between functionality and privacy.</p><p><b>The SeekPrivacy Shield</b><br>SeekPrivacy is built on the principle that the phone's operating system and other apps should be considered "hostile territory." The architecture assumes the Android OS is compromised and every other app is a potential spy. Even if a malicious app gains full control of the device, your data remains a cryptographic black box.</p><p>SeekPrivacy eliminates this blackmail. It provides multi-layered defense by isolating data within an encrypted sandbox, inaccessible to other apps, the OS, or unauthorized physical extraction. This provides activist-grade security with the belief: "Privacy Without the Tradeoff of Ease." Even if a malicious app has full device control, it sees nothing.</p><p><b>Core Defense Features:</b></p><ul><li><b>Anti-Forensic Shredder:</b> Deletion triggers a RandomAccessFile wipe of the first 1MB, destroying file headers to defeat forensic recovery tools.</li><li><b>Hardware-Locked Vault:</b> Keys are anchored to the physical TEE hardware; without the specific gateway, data remains cryptographically locked even if the phone is "unlocked."</li><li><b>Trauma-Resilient Recovery:</b> A world-first recovery bridge using the physical ANDROID_ID as a hardware anchor, ensuring access even if credentials falter under extreme duress.</li><li><b>Zero-Persistence Logic:</b> Manual memory zeroing of sensitive keys and FLAG_SECURE hardware blocks against screen-scraping and mirroring.</li><li><b><File Categorization:</b> Nested Sub-Folders | Instant search | Rename & Move | Live Count</li></ul>
